Workshops

Holding workshops with clients is vital in understanding the reasons they’re embarking on a project. This allows us to define the crucial elements for their projects’ success. To do this, we gather as many stakeholders as needed and discuss proposals and areas for research. This allows us to define the expected outcomes for each project.

Brand strategy

Understanding how consumers think we are able to champion their needs when they can’t be there themselves. Through research we provide insights and analytics which can be applied to strategies creating engaging experiences that keep consumers coming back.

Product management

Having a defined product roadmap and the concept of a minimum viable product are key to successful product management. This helps define each MVP by what should be included. This allows us to prioritise the next set of features to come, enabling successful deliverables.

Risk assessment

For any project it’s vital to identify the complexities and the greatest risks involved. We look at a project and determining the areas that are the most uncertainty. The goal of risk assessment is a risk mitigation plan. This helps reduce production time by revealing potential obstacles early on and reducing bugs and defects.

Gap analysis

Gaps are the areas between where we are in development and where we want to be. Understanding these helps us strategise the best path to overcome obstacles. Knowing which areas are in need of attention allows us to focus our resources and maximise our time. Setting milestones and target dates helps keep everything on track.

Project planning

Project planners understand the moving parts of a work in process and develop strategies to keep on track. They do this over the course of a project and adjust it as necessary; tracking the team’s progress toward the goal: an engaging consumer experience, delivered within budget and on time.

Competitor analysis

By analysing a clients’ competition, we see what makes our clients stand out. Understanding what’s out there and already working, as well as the things that could work but aren't used yet, we can help find a clients USP and develop that into a strategy to deliver great brand experiences.

Great UX requires empathy. By researching and thinking like the end user we ensure that your service is intuitive, accessible, and memorable.

By understanding the project’s audience and how they will interact with the brand, UX allows us to craft an exquisite experience. By streamlining key interactions it allows users to find what they need effortlessly making any interaction with the company pleasurable.

User Research

Designing desirable experiences starts with the user. Through a range of user research methods we ensure that your project is fine-tuned and tailored to your audience.

Personas

By representing a specific user group in a persona, we humanise complex data and help give design teams a consistent focus and design target.

Journey maps

A journey map is a visualisation of the process that a person goes through to achieve a goal. A good user journey map will include omni-channel interactions with the brand allowing us to identify pain points, areas for improvements and opportunities.

Sitemaps

Every site or app needs an overall high-level hierarchy for organizing information. Sitemaps, as these are called, take best practices for usability—as well as clients’ and end users’ particular needs—into consideration.

Wireframes

Wireframes act as a guide for both design and development teams. They state key areas of functionality, communication, and user interaction. This allows each team member to understand the navigation, interactions, and functionality of each screen or interaction.

Clickable prototypes

A user interface prototype is a hypothesis — a possible design solution that you consider for a specific design problem. Creating a clickable prototype allows us to watch users interact with the system before development. This means that issues can be dealt with earlier in the design stages preventing unnecessary work.

When used sparingly, design trends and gimmicks can make services standout. However, everything must be of some use to the user and not detract from their ultimate goal. We endeavour to design memorable brand experiences that enhance a user's interaction.

Brand identity

Brand identity is the life blood of a company. It's the primary element users relate to an experience and should convey the essence of the brand quickly and efficiently. It also needs to be as versatile yet timeless, adapting to suit a company’s goals. We conduct thorough research to direct everything from a brand’s logo and typeface to its tone of voice.

Responsive interfaces

Responsive interfaces are designs that change the appearance of an interface based upon the current screen size and orientation of a user's device. This allows for a more accessible solution for user's.

Interaction design

Interaction design is the dialogue between a person and a product, system, or service. This manifests in both the physical and emotional interplay between form, function, and technology as the user experiences it over time.

Animation

Moving elements are a powerful tool to not only attract users’ attention but tell a brand's story. It's important to consider and animation's goal, its frequency of occurrence, and its mechanics so as not to be distracting or annoying.

Style guides

Style guides are a collection of all the elements in a product’s user interface. This enforces consistency across any of the brand's platforms building upon the brand identity.

Copyediting
